
I have realised  how important it is to know you body, yourself,  and to know the women around you do too. I spent too much time in fear of what my body was and could bring me to for so long I saw it too intensely as a limitation…

Letizia Pipitone’s activism  was a reclamation of the female body and translating vulnerability into collective action. Her fight was intensely focused on the physical realities of being a woman. Through her profound connection to the 150-hour courses on women's health, her activism centred on bodily self-awareness, tearing down medical taboos, and taking militant action—like occupying the S. Anna hospital—to demand humane reproductive care.



I remember an immense unease, an acute discomfort  at the thought of a female body, a body called mine  by those who mine were not

I remember the hiding places of a distant little girl who did not yet understand the permanence of the parts she hated, and which were watched by those who did not know her.

I remember, later, the grown attempts to uncover it, that cursed body, and I remember the fear of listening to it.

But then I met a singular woman, and I remember the femininity she exalted, and I remember the way I fell in love before the mirror of a dim room.

I do not truly know what defines me as  woman, but a woman I am, and woman I love with arduous passion.

The woman I am, I love her more than rest;  hungry for life, she leans out onto the balcony,  frightened by some wretched banality,  accused of every wrong  by herself, by her foremothers.

The woman I am, I am her through dreams and desires, a star-filled mind. Eternally watched by blind eyes, mine and yours.

Gracefully I laugh within this woman, 
as a man, freedom would be born with a breath, 
and yet, woman as I am 
the dream of it gladdens all the more
