I remember praying every night, for them stop seeing me so small, I remember praying all night for them to start being my friends, I remember praying so hard for them to leave you alone, I remember praying and  I remember how we tried so hard to let them forget us, I remember when I stopped praying and you were there and we were finally alone.


Renata Bonino had a faith-driven empathy that transformed personal fear into protective leadership. Her activism was fuelled by a deeply held Catholic belief in social justice. Despite her own profound insecurities and fear of confrontation, she found the courage to lead spontaneous strikes and face down arrogant directors specifically to protect her exhausted, overworked female colleagues from exploitation.

and you were there 
and we were finally alone.


Ghosts! and the sky falls mute,
and the bed falls mute,
and the footsteps fall mute

ghosts! shady dwellers of my heart
gather at the front
where my little soldiers live.

A dreadful pity, Dear God,
and I, blasphemy
of your petrified soul,
chase the hem of death;
between white fingers, these shards of glass,
between red limbs, these wounds.

We are not such enemies after all:
you, man of all;
I, woman of myself
myself and half again.

My flesh trembles beneath
the weight of feminine will
and my frail illusions
of an androgynous being.

Yet in this vast solitude,
where the head falls upon my lap,
desperately, I brush against my memories
and call a thousand names
and you, God,
you are but one among them.
