Domenica Airaudo’s friendship and solidarity with the women in her department  kept me nostalgic all through my reading,  m for I had a similar friendship with a girl back in school.Together we kept inventing new spaces around us, we had a unbreakable hope in the people  and what they could do, every act was for us an act of creation.

Domenica Airaudo’s activism was deeply rooted in building localised, tight-knit alliances. She united the women in the Purchasing Department to fight the systemic downgrading of female typists, transforming everyday office proximity into an organised demand for professional equality.


I REMEMBER SHAPING WORLDS AND SHAPING THOSE WHO LIVED IN IT, I REMEMBER THE SISTERHOOD WE SHARED, WE SHARE. 
I REMBER WHERE FRIENDSHIP LEADS THE HEARTS, AND I REMEMBER US BEING WOMEN TOGETHER.


You are of a wandering sadness,  drift a little in my heart, for its foam comes from yours.
Rest, my friend,  your violent unraveling does not frighten me—  but you do, 
where are you?

You and I kept ourselves intact  in the limbo of hope,  strange little girls,  and you know me,  I know you,  and is that not anguish enough already?

But I understand you  in the crow perched upon that balcony  that too little I listened to,  though it was an accomplice of the sky.
In the crow I understand you,  and in the sound that carries it away.

I know you;  the shadow of our friendship  cradles my sleep.

Dear friend, come here,  the bed is wide  and life will arrive soon enough.
Life arrives,  for it follows you faithfully.

Come here,  I wait for you  as one waits  for a sister.
